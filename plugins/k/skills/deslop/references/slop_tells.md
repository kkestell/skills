# Slop Tells

The signal is _density_, not single words in isolation. A lone "however" or "robust" is often fine. Multiple tells clustered in one paragraph are what make docs read as AI-generated.

## Lexical Tells

- **Investigative filler verbs:** `delve`, `explore`, `navigate`, `examine`, `study` when they add no meaning beyond "this section is about X"
- **Puffed-up demonstrative verbs:** `showcase`, `underscore`, `highlight`, `illuminate`, `elucidate`
- **Corporate operational verbs:** `leverage`, `utilize`, `align`, `augment`, `foster`, `facilitate`, `streamline`, `optimize`, `empower`, `spearhead`
- **Abstract metaphor nouns:** `landscape`, `realm`, `tapestry`, `mosaic`, `labyrinth`, `paradigm`, `ecosystem`, `journey`, `intersection`
- **Revealing metaphors:** `unveiling the layers`, `deep dive`, `unlocking the potential`, `shedding light on`, `peeling back the curtain`
- **Corporate superlatives without evidence:** `crucial`, `vital`, `robust`, `seamless`, `dynamic`, `transformative`, `innovative`, `cutting-edge`, `world-class`, `state-of-the-art`
- **Transitional padding:** `Moreover`, `Furthermore`, `Consequently`, `Additionally`, `Undoubtedly`, `It's worth noting that`, `It's important to note that`

## Syntactic Tells

- **Corrective antithesis:** `It is not just X, it is Y` or `This isn't about X; it's about Y`
- **Superficial rule of three:** triads chosen for rhythm rather than meaning
- **Outline conclusions:** `In conclusion`, `Ultimately`, `In summary`, `A testament to`
- **Sweeping philosophical endings:** `continue to shape the future`, `presents both challenges and opportunities`, `in an ever-changing world`
- **Negative parallelisms:** `Not just X, but also Y` when habitual
- **Copulative avoidance:** `stands as a`, `serves as a`, `represents a` where plain `is` would do
- **Low burstiness:** every sentence has the same length and complexity across a paragraph

## Typographical Tells

- **Aggressive markdown formatting:** bullet lists for prose, headers for single sentences, bold on every other word
- **Stray emoji:** emoji in technical doc headers or bullets
- **Em dash abuse:** multiple em dashes per paragraph to force drama
- **Semicolon saturation:** unnaturally frequent semicolons in otherwise ordinary docs

## Domain-Specific Tells

- **README slop:** `Whether you're a beginner or an experienced developer`, `This project aims to`, `Getting started is easy!`
- **API doc slop:** `This powerful method allows you to`, `easily configure`
- **Tutorial slop:** `So, what exactly is X?`, `Let's dive in!`
- **Changelog slop:** `We're excited to announce`

## Calibration Notes

- Technical precision beats style heuristics. `Robust to network partitions` is a technical claim, not a puff word.
- Casual tone is not the problem. Empty tone is.
- Prefer pointing to the smallest rewrite that restores trust.
